Build things right. And build the right things.

Computer Science taught me how to build for machines. But an eclectic mix of classmates from engineering, music, theatre, journalism, design, and business taught me how to build for people.

People & Machines: it’s a relationship I’ve cultivated for over 15 years, and one I’m lucky to be designing today at Punchcut. As a design lead and technologist, I’ve guided exceptional teams of interaction, visual, and motion designers in shaping the future of fitness, automotive, finance, and education experiences. I’ve also had the privilege of mentoring new designers from University of Cincinnati, Rhode Island School of Design, and Brigham Young University.

But while my career shifted from software development to design, I still believe deeply in exercising code as a creative medium. I made this site to share my personal work and thoughts on interactive media.
